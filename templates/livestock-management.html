Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our livestock management system

What types of livestock does your system support?

Our system is designed to work with all major livestock types including cattle (dairy and beef), pigs, sheep, goats, and poultry. The hardware and software are customized based on the specific needs of each animal type.

How durable are the RFID tags and health monitoring devices?

Our RFID tags and monitoring devices are designed specifically for harsh farm environments. They are water-resistant, dust-proof, and built to withstand impacts. The RFID tags have a 5+ year lifespan, while the health monitoring collars typically last 3-4 years with proper maintenance.

Can the system integrate with my existing farm management software?

Yes, our Enterprise plan includes API access that allows for integration with most popular farm management software. We currently have direct integrations with FarmERP, Agrivi, and Farmbrite, with more being added regularly. Our team can also develop custom integrations for specific needs.

How accurate is the health monitoring system?

Our health monitoring system has been validated in multiple university studies with an accuracy rate of over 92% for detecting common health issues 24-48 hours before visible symptoms appear. The system continuously improves through machine learning as more data is collected.

Do I need internet connectivity in my barns or pastures?

Our system uses a mesh network of base stations that can operate with minimal connectivity. You'll need internet at your main farm office, but the base stations can relay data from remote areas. For very large operations, we offer satellite connectivity options for areas without cellular coverage.

How much time does it take to implement the system?

Basic implementation can be completed in 1-2 days for small operations. For medium to large operations, we recommend a phased approach over 1-2 weeks. Our team provides comprehensive training and support throughout the implementation process to ensure a smooth transition.
